AI-Edited Image Detector

A deep learning system that detects and segments AI-edited regions in images, helping identify manipulation, inpainting, and synthetic alterations.

Mission

Our mission is to build a reliable and interpretable model that can identify images manipulated using AI inpainting or generative editing techniques. As synthetic media becomes more pervasive, tools like this help promote transparency, trust, and safety across digital platforms.

About the Project

This project focuses on detecting AI-generated edits in images by training a segmentation model on multiple inpainting datasets including Beyond the Brush, InpaintCOCO, and COCO-Inpaint. Edited regions tend to have subtle artifacts such as texture inconsistencies, unnatural boundaries, or model hallucination patterns.

We trained a U-Net architecture with a ResNet-18 encoder to segment edited regions pixel-by-pixel. The model was optimized using IoU and F-score, achieving strong performance across a wide range of mask sizes. Additional metrics including per-mask-size IoU and precision/recall were used to evaluate robustness against extremely small or large edited areas.

This work demonstrates how computer vision techniques can be applied to improve digital media authenticity, support misinformation defense strategies, and help users identify AI-modified content.
