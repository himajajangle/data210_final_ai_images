Datasets

Beyond the Brush Dataset
  • 4,930 image-mask pairs
  • 2,459 medium masks + 2,471 large masks
  • Train/Val/Test Split: 60-20-20
  • Mean mask coverage: 22.1% of pixels
  • Range: 0.1% - 59.9%

Key Insight: Small masks (<10% coverage) were excluded to focus on medium and large manipulations.

COCO Inpaint Dataset
  • 1,260 images with varying mask sizes
  • Diverse manipulation scenarios
  • Real-world object insertions/removals

Image Augmentation

To train the model to handle noisy, low-quality, real-world images — not just clean lab data — we applied augmentations including rotation, blur, compression, and combinations thereof.

Model Architecture: U-Net vs. ViT

U-Net with ResNet-18
  • Classic segmentation architecture
  • ResNet-18 encoder (ImageNet pretrained)
  • Excels at precise boundary localization
  • Dropout: 20%
  • Threshold: 50%
Vision Transformer (ViT) ⭐
  • Transformer-based architecture
  • Captures global context across entire image
  • Self-attention detects subtle inconsistencies
  • Better at identifying manipulation patterns
  • Dropout: 15%
  • Threshold: 36%
Final Model: ViT achieved 9% improvement in F-Score over U-Net

Key Decisions in Model Development

  1. Why we added dropout: Initial U-Net showed overfitting; dropout improved validation performance
  2. Why we switched to ViT: U-Net plateaued at IoU ~0.82; ViT's attention mechanism achieved better results
  3. Why we lowered threshold (50% → 36%): We were missing small manipulated regions; 36% improved recall while maintaining precision
